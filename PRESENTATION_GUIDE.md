## üí¨ Q&A Preparation

### Expected Questions & Answers

**Q: "How is this different from Slack or Microsoft Teams?"**

**A:** "Great question. Slack and Teams are platforms - they lock you in. We're platform-agnostic. We connect to Slack, Teams, WhatsApp, Signal, and more. Think of us as the AI layer on top of all your existing tools. Plus, we're built on Matrix.org, an open protocol, so no vendor lock-in."

---

**Q: "What's your competitive advantage? Why can't Slack just add AI?"**

**A:** "Three reasons:
1. **Platform Neutrality:** We work with ALL platforms, not competing with them
2. **Open Protocol:** Built on Matrix.org, which big tech can't control
3. **AI-First:** Our entire architecture is designed around AI - it's not a bolt-on feature
Slack adding AI is like adding a sunroof to a car. We're building a flying car."

---

**Q: "How do you handle privacy and security?"**

**A:** "Privacy is our foundation:
- Built on Matrix.org open protocol with E2E encryption
- Self-hosted option for enterprises
- No message storage on our servers (relay only)
- GDPR compliant by design
- Zero-knowledge architecture
Unlike big tech, we can't read your messages even if we wanted to."

---

**Q: "What's your go-to-market strategy?"**

**A:** "Three-phased approach:
1. **Developer Community (Month 1-3):** Launch on Product Hunt, Hacker News. Developers love open source + AI.
2. **Remote Workers (Month 4-6):** Partner with remote work communities, indie hackers. They feel the pain most.
3. **Enterprise Sales (Month 7-12):** Bottom-up adoption, then enterprise packages. Slack's playbook.
We're starting where the pain is highest."

---

**Q: "How much does the AI cost per user?"**

**A:** "Great question - we've optimized this:
- GPT-3.5 for classification: $0.002 per user/day
- GPT-4 for daily summary: $0.05 per user/day
- Whisper for transcription: $0.006 per minute
Average: $2.50/user/month AI cost. We charge $9.99, so 75% gross margin."

---

**Q: "What if OpenAI raises prices or becomes unavailable?"**

**A:** "We've planned for this:
1. Multi-model support (can switch to Claude, PaLM)
2. Self-hosted AI option for enterprises
3. Freemium model means we can absorb some cost
4. Volume discounts as we scale
5. Eventually train our own domain-specific models
AI is a feature, not our moat. Our moat is the unified protocol and network effects."

---

**Q: "How do you plan to acquire users?"**

**A:** "Product-led growth strategy:
- **Free tier:** Unlimited basic aggregation
- **Viral loops:** Share summaries, invite team
- **Content marketing:** Blog about productivity
- **SEO:** Target 'unified messaging' keywords
- **Partnerships:** Integrate with tools people use
- **Community:** Active on Reddit, Twitter, Discord
First 10K users organically, then paid channels."

---

**Q: "What's your biggest risk?"**

**A:** "Honest answer: Platform API changes. If WhatsApp or Slack restrict API access, we adapt:
1. We're building on Matrix.org (can't be shut down)
2. Multiple platforms, not dependent on one
3. Email integration as fallback
4. Self-hosted option bypasses APIs
5. Working with platforms, not against them
We've designed for resilience from day one."

- Working product (not slides)
